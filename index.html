Is the cafeteria food really bad or
are we just not aware of our needs?
Madhavi Sharma
Information Design
9 out of 10 students at the National Institute of Design,
Bangalore feel like their nutritional requirements are not being met....
They eat at the campus cafeteria at least 3-4 times a day, and don’t want to opt for food from outside due to a variety of reasons which range from the mess food being convenient & accessible to the fact that they have already paid for it and don’t want to spend extra for food outside to not having cooking facilities set up in their apartments.
Is the menu really lacking in nutritious food? Are there other factors which are preventing us from making the most out of the available food in the mess?
Let’s have a deep dive into the cafeteria menu served on a typical Wednesday.
...and the typical amount of calories and macronutrients present in its constituents
  • Healthier options at snack time such as choosing corn over french fries can help with calorie regulation.
  • Protein requirements are being met sufficiently for non-vegetarians on days that chicken is served. Chicken is only served on Wednesdays at Lunch. For vegetarians and for all other days, dal curry, buttermilk, eggs may be consumed to meet protein requirements.
  • Contrary to complains about the lack of items containing fiber, there are enough items to suffice the daily fiber requirements.
  • A single boiled egg constitutes 55% of the daily cholesterol requirement.
Having a good understanding of the nutritional breakdown of the food we consume on a daily basis and the requirements of our bodies can serve as a powerful tool to help regulate our eating habits.
